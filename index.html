Three leaks.
Every load.

There still isn’t purpose-built software for car-hauler execution—that’s the gap. These failures repeat because nothing runs in the cab with the driver.

01
The Yard
Loading from memory, every time
💸$150–$300 lost when a vehicle doesn't fit and is left behind

The driver receives a paper load sheet. No guidance on configuration, no weight check, no verification. When something goes wrong, vehicles get unloaded and reloaded. What should take 90 minutes takes half a day.

02
The Hunt
Navigating the yard without a route
⏱20–45 minutes lost per load walking the yard

Bay codes on the load sheet are positions scattered across a facility spanning several city blocks. Some yards have a basic diagram. Most have painted rows on asphalt. None give the driver an optimized pickup route for their specific load.

03
The Delivery
Arriving blind at the dealer
🗺15–30 minutes lost per stop at an unfamiliar dealer

The load sheet gives an address — not the entrance, the driveway, or how to position an 80-foot rig in a tight street. Experienced drivers know from memory. New drivers guess. Every wrong approach is income off the next run.
